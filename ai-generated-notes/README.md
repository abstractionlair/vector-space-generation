## Do not cite as evidence

These documents were produced from a small probe (~4 prompts, N=2-4 vectors
before discretization) and were dressed in formal research-paper language.
In particular:

- The write-ups overstate secondary observations (e.g. "instruction-tuning
  brittleness") into headline conclusions. The author deliberately did **not**
  headline these in the top-level `README.md` — the headline there is the
  negative result that the vectors appear to be superpositions of distinct
  next-token candidates, not a richer continuous language.
- The reviews are LLM opinions on the implementation, not independent human
  audit. They repeat and package the same observations presented more
  cautiously in the author's own writing.

## What to read instead

The authoritative narrative is the top-level [`README.md`](../README.md).
The actual experimental outputs are the raw console captures in
[`conversations_and_artifacts/`](../conversations_and_artifacts/) (the

[`docs/`](../docs/) — those are spec/notes written by the author, not LLM
output.

output look better or worse. They were moved out of `results/` and `docs/`
